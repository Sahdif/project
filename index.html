BEBO - The Failing Success

What is Bebo?

When talking about social media apps and websites, we usally think of YouTube, Facebook and Twitter. However, Bebo was another social media platform that was competing these giants and the time it was made in 2005. Founded by couple Michael Birch and Xochi Birch who aimed to compete with the social media giants.

What's Special About It?

At the time, there was another company named AOL looking to enter the social media race and eyed Bebo in an attempt to do it. They offered the couple an amazing $850M to buy out the company in which the offer was accepted.

Altough it sounded like everything was ready for AOL to make a big scene in the social media world, Bebo was just not able to compete with the other platforms and eventually flopped and plummeted in value. Reports state that AOL sold it to digital media investors Criterion Capital Partners in 2010 for a reported $10 million.

What's amazing is that in 2013, Bebo was bought by none other than the founders themselves for a shocking $1M. They expressed their interest in attempting to turn Bebo into a product that people will use like how they invisioned all the way back in 2005
